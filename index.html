Im Hinterhaus

This project was created as part of the "Architecture and light" theme. An analysis of the building MFH Landskronhof was the starting material for the final video. How is light reflected in the building based on form and function? When creating our 3D models, we were inspired by the original building and focussed on surface structures, building elements and mood.
The building is located in a backyard surrounded by urban houses. The tranquillity there and the interaction of the facade with the surroundings are surprising. A multitude of transitions, which became part of our animation.

Paper Plants

See More

In this photographic study of an Alocasia, we analysed form, colour and light in detail. The output of this project was three series. One of them with speed light, another with steady light and the third a combination of the two in the form of a superimposition. It was particularly exciting to see how form, colour and structures behave differently. Among other things, the ability to create a serial composition was promoted.
In the second part of the project, we created a virtual room with our superimposed images and our plant. We chose a broken flower pot as a shell for our room. This forms a threshold between dead and alive, unstable and stable. By changing the shape of the superimposed images and overlaying them, resulting in a glitch, we created a dystopian world that can be placed in the real world.

Digital Design Processes

See More

With the content keyword "Plant-based" I created two different series, each with a formal keyword. The first series was given the word "Dynamics" and the second the word "loud". In the first series, I moved a branch through a scanner and analysed the resulting designs for movement and abstraction. For the second series, I used different leaves as stamps and applied different amounts of colour to the paper. It was also very exciting to look at the different leaf structures.
Towards the end, the focus was on organising all the designs according to certain criteria. I laid out all the drafts in analogue form. This resulted in matrices, which I converted into digital form.

Creating an immersive experience with the use of typography and space. This was the concept of our project "Typographic staging in space". The word "cut-out" inspired us to use a projector on various surfaces. By cutting out areas of the illuminated surface and playing different animations, we created a live performance. The people watching could become part of this immersive experience and walk through the installation.

Let's print the
error

See More

In our project "LET'S PRINT THE ERROR!", we looked at how "errors" occur when printing three-dimensionally modelled chairs. The term "error" is used here in inverted commas, as the machine only executes the command it receives and the output does not represent a real error. The error itself is caused by human expectations.
During the practical work we analysed the limits of using support structures for printing. We also printed the models in different orientations, which gave us different results. This experiment was exciting because the orientation is only important in analogue and not in digital.
To push the limits of digital, we tested different materials and textures on our 3D models in Blender, experiments that can only exist in this form in the digital world.

ROTBOD

See More

In this project, rotating letters were used. I worked in a photo studio and tried to make letters appear three-dimensional through long exposures. I used different colour foils to create different moods. The focus was on the production of a moving poster that advertises the final exhibition of the module Animation/Illustration at the School of Art Basel.
The title ROTBOD stands for Rotation Body and creates a link between an object and it's interaction with the environment. Just how movement becomes an important aspect in animation.

Branch

See More

This corporate design was about creating a communication platform. The name of my platform is "Branch". It aims to connect people who have mental health problems or concerns. Be it with people with similar problems or with specialists. In addition to a chat function, there is also the opportunity to find out about certain specific topics. In addition to education and information, there is the possibility of anonymity and the obligation of verification by specialists.
The name "Branch" and the colour scheme are intended to give hope for the possibility of development and to promote progress in overcoming mental health problems.

About Me

I am a Graphic Designer and Digital Creator based in Basel, Switzerland, currently studying Visual Communication and Digital Spaces in the Bachelor program. I started my studies at the Academy of Art and Design Basel, Institute Digital Communication Environments, in September 2023.

My Workflow

During my design process, I love to keep a good structure. Starting with a simple sketch of several ideas leading to an organised concept, helps me to take further steps. I always want to exchange ideas and initial practical implementations with other people and clients to get more inspirations concerning the project I'm working on.

My Style

Throughout the development of my career I started to work in a more classic and minimalistic graphical style. However, as a contemporary artist, I love to keep my mind open for new experiences.
